## Overview

As a <span className="highlighted">Full-Stack Engineer I</span>, I helped build several
full-stack web and mobile applications across a handful of industries to be used by
hundreds to thousands of users.

The primary stack I worked with in this position was
<span className="highlighted">TypeScript, React, Node.js, PostgreSQL/Prisma, and React Native</span>.

In this position I often worked with UI/UX designers, project managers, and clients
in an <span className="highlighted">Agile environment</span> to capture necessary requirements
and deliver high-quality results in a timely manner. My responsibilities included
building user-facing interfaces, fixing/reporting user-submitted bugs, designing
<span className="highlighted">RESTful APIs</span>, and integrating with PostgreSQL databases.

This was my first professional
<span className="highlighted">full-stack engineering role</span>, and the value and experience
it provided me cannot be understated.

I witnessed first-hand the
<span className="highlighted">full project life-cycle</span>, from ideation and discovery all
the way to launch and post-launch support. I gained a thorough understanding of
technologies I had not yet worked with extensively, like TypeScript and PostgreSQL,
and received insight on <span className="highlighted">industry best practices</span> and
real-world implementations through code reviews and war rooms with senior developers.

Stepping into this role was initially intimidating, as it was my first real,
non-personal foray into full-stack development, but as I gained my footing in this
position I became much more comfortable with navigating
<span className="highlighted">real-world codebases</span> and writing
<span className="highlighted">high-quality, convention-following code</span> alongside other
developers.
